## Purpose
This document defines what Pink Halo should sell, how products should be grouped, how inventory should be planned, and how the store should avoid random products that do not fit the brand.

## Product Rule
Every product must match at least one of these ideas: feminine, flattering, comfortable, photo-ready, giftable, seasonal, or useful for building a complete outfit.

## Core Product Categories

1. Dresses: casual dresses, brunch dresses, event dresses, maxi dresses, mini dresses, and seasonal dresses.
2. Tops: blouses, fitted tops, basics, sweaters, graphic tops, and going-out tops.
3. Bottoms: skirts, jeans, shorts, trousers, and matching set bottoms.
4. Lounge: matching sets, pajamas, robes, cozy pants, hoodies, and homewear.
5. Accessories: jewelry, handbags, hair clips, sunglasses, belts, and beauty-adjacent fashion accessories.
6. Seasonal: holiday outfits, summer drops, vacation pieces, fall comfort, and limited collections.

## Starter Inventory Strategy
Start with a small controlled catalog instead of buying too many random products. A strong first catalog could include 20 to 40 products across dresses, tops, lounge sets, and accessories.

## First Collection Recommendation
The first Pink Halo collection should be small, clear, and easy to market. A good starting launch is 20 to 40 products total, not hundreds.

## Suggested First Product Mix

- Dresses: 5 to 8 items
- Tops: 5 to 8 items
- Lounge or matching sets: 4 to 6 items
- Accessories: 8 to 12 items
- Seasonal or limited items: 2 to 4 items

## Product Selection Scorecard

Each product should be scored before purchase:

- Does it match the Pink Halo look?
- Can it be photographed well?
- Is the margin strong enough?
- Is the supplier reliable?
- Does it fit a collection?
- Can it be bundled with another item?
- Would it work in TikTok or Pinterest content?
- Does it solve a real outfit need?

## Inventory Fields To Track
Product name, SKU, category, collection, supplier, cost, shipping cost, selling price, margin, sizes, colors, reorder point, product photo status, listing status, launch date, performance notes.

## Inventory Risk Control
Do not overbuy before proof of demand. Start with small quantities or sample-based validation. Restock only after sales, customer feedback, and content performance support the decision.
